# Reference Note: Liu et al. 2026 Biodiversity and Conservation

## Citation

Liu, J., Kang, Y. & Jiang, A. (2026). Camera trap-based spatial patterns and drivers of mammal taxonomic, functional, and phylogenetic diversity in Mainland China. *Biodiversity and Conservation*, 35, Article 33. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10531-025-03248-x

## Why It Matters for TW-CVII

This paper is a national-scale camera-trap synthesis that aligns closely with the database architecture needed for TW-CVII. It is especially relevant for:

- nationwide camera-trap literature compilation;
- taxonomic, functional and phylogenetic diversity fields;
- protected-area versus non-protected comparisons;
- trait and evolutionary-history layers for functional intactness;
- methodological benchmarking against a 281-paper synthesis.

## Extraction-Relevant Facts From Springer Preview

- Publication date: 10 January 2026.
- Dataset scope reported in abstract: 281 published articles from 2012-2022.
- Taxonomic scope reported in abstract: 210 terrestrial mammal species.
- Spatial scope reported in abstract: 212 mainland China sites.
- Article reports higher diversity dimensions within protected areas and links diversity to terrain ruggedness and area.
- Data availability notes a Figshare dataset DOI: https://doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.28972310, with embargo language on the Springer page.

## TW-CVII Table Mapping

- `source`: article metadata, DOI, subscription/data-embargo status.
- `study`: national literature synthesis design.
- `site`: 212 site units if the dataset becomes available.
- `species_inventory`: site-level mammal inventory.
- `functional_traits`: traits used for functional diversity.
- `taxonomy`: standardized species names across the literature corpus.
- `protected_area_context`: protected-area flag, protected-area identity and site area.
- `environmental_covariates`: terrain ruggedness, area, climate, human pressure and land-cover drivers if available.
- `derived_index`: taxonomic, functional and phylogenetic diversity metrics.
